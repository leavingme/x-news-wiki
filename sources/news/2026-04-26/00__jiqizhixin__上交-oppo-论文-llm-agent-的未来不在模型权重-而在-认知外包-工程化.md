# 上交 + OPPO 论文：LLM Agent 的未来不在模型权重，而在「认知外包」工程化

## 摘要

**一句话核心判断：LLM Agent 的下一代竞争是外部工具链的工程深度，而非模型参数规模。**

- 上海交大与 OPPO 联合团队提出「外部化（externalization）」概念——将记忆、技能、会话规则等认知负担从模型内部转移到可靠的外部工具箱。
- 核心逻辑：复杂认知问题（如长程记忆、多技能编排）用内部权重解决是低效的，将其转化为结构化的外部操作步骤，成本更低、可靠性更高。
- 论文隐含的产业信号：手机厂商（OPPO）和学术机构（交大）联手探索「模型+外部 scaffolding」的协同范式，可能是未来端侧 Agent 的主流架构方向。

gakki 的锐评：这篇论文的实质是「认知分工」从隐喻变成工程学科——以后评价一个 Agent 好不好，不仅要看模型多强，更要看它把多少认知负担外包给了外部系统，这对现有的「模型即 Agent」叙事是一次降维冲击。

## 原文

Think building an AI agent is just a better brain? What if the real secret is what you add outside the model?

A team from Shanghai Jiao Tong University, OPPO, and others argues that the future of LLM agents isn't about bigger weights, but smarter scaffolding.

They introduce a concept called "externalization" —moving hard tasks like memory, skills, and conversation rules out of the model's brain and into a reliable external toolbox. This turns messy cognitive problems into clear, solvable steps.

Their review shows that agent infrastructure (memory stores, reusable skills, and protocols) consistently outperforms relying solely on the model's internal knowledge, particularly in long-term tasks and complex coordination.

The takeaway: The smartest agent isn't the biggest model; it's the one with the best support system.
