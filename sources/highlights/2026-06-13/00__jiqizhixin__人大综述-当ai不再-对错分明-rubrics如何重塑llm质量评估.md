# 人大综述：当AI不再"对错分明"，Rubrics如何重塑LLM质量评估

## 摘要

**要点：**

gakki锐评：当AI能力扩展到无法简单判错的研究/医疗/Agent领域，Rubrics是从"打分"到"标准定义"的关键基础设施——这也是Anthropic Safety评估体系的方法论根源。

## 原文

How do you evaluate an AI that writes research, diagnoses diseases, or uses tools—when “right or wrong” no longer cuts it?

Researchers from Renmin University of China (Liu et al.) surveyed the emerging use of rubrics for LLMs. Rubrics are structured checklists that break down quality into criteria like factuality, safety, reasoning, and utility—turning vague preferences into clear, trainable standards.

The survey maps all major rubric approaches: how to build them (direct, contrastive, iterative, co-evolving), how to use them to train policy models and reward models, and how they improve evaluation across general and domain-specific tasks. It also flags open problems like rubric hacking, bias, and personalization.

A must-read for anyone building or judging the next generation of AI systems.
